# Features: Family Law Marketing Website

## Table Stakes (Must Have)

These features are expected on any professional law firm website. Missing these signals "not legitimate."

### 1. Contact Options
- **Prominent phone number** - Visible in header, clickable on mobile
- **Contact form** - 3-4 fields max (name, phone, email, brief message)
- **Above the fold placement** - Both phone and form visible without scrolling

### 2. Trust Signals
- **Firm name and branding** - Professional logo/wordmark
- **Practice areas** - Clear list of family law services offered
- **Professional imagery** - High-quality photos or illustrations
- **Attorney introduction** - Even brief bio builds human connection

### 3. Content Sections
- **Hero section** - Headline that acknowledges visitor's situation
- **Services overview** - What the firm handles
- **Why choose us** - Differentiators (experience, approach, results)
- **Location/service area** - Where the firm practices

### 4. Technical Requirements
- **Mobile responsive** - 60%+ of legal traffic is mobile
- **Fast loading** - Under 3 seconds
- **SSL certificate** - HTTPS required for trust and SEO
- **Accessible** - Proper contrast, readable fonts (16px+)

## Differentiators (Nice to Have)

These features can increase conversions but aren't strictly required for v1.

### 1. Enhanced Trust
- **Client testimonials** - 73% of clients trust firms with 4+ star ratings
- **Case results** - Where ethically permitted
- **Bar admissions/credentials** - Specific certifications
- **Video introduction** - Increases conversions 80-86%

### 2. Conversion Boosters
- **Live chat** - For after-hours lead capture
- **Downloadable guide** - "Divorce Guide" etc. for email capture
- **FAQ section** - Answers common questions, reduces tire-kickers
- **Sticky mobile contact bar** - Follows as user scrolls

### 3. Content Depth
- **Detailed practice area descriptions** - Each service explained
- **Attorney bios with photos** - Humanizes the firm
- **Blog/articles** - Good for SEO, shows expertise
- **Process explanation** - What clients can expect

## Anti-Features (Deliberately Exclude)

| Feature | Why Exclude |
|---------|-------------|
| Multi-page navigation | User explicitly requested single-page |
| Client portal/login | Marketing site only, not an app |
| Complex animations | Distracts from conversion goal |
| Auto-play video | Annoying, uses mobile data |
| Long forms (7+ fields) | Every field drops completion ~11% |
| Buried contact info | Phone must be immediately visible |
| Generic stock photos | Feels impersonal, reduces trust |
